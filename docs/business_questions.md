# Project Plan: Business Questions to Solve

Before I start writing SQL or building dashboards, I need to know what problems I'm actually trying to solve. These are the big questions that will keep me from getting lost in the data.

## 1. Is our growth "healthy"?
Sales numbers look great, but the support team is seeing a lot of bad reviews. I need to figure out if we are growing too fast and making people unhappy. If sales are up but ratings are down, we're in trouble.

## 2. Is this real growth or just holiday luck?
Our revenue goes up and down like crazy. Is the business actually getting bigger, or are we just getting a temporary boost from events like Black Friday? I need to look at our normal, day-to-day sales to find the real trend.

## 3. Why don't people come back?
We have thousands of customers, but most only buy one thing and disappear. Finding new customers is expensive, so I need to find out why they aren't loyal. I'll check if a bad first experience is what's scaring them off.

## 4. Which categories are hurting our reputation?
Some product categories might bring in a lot of cash but cause even more headaches. I want to find the categories that have the highest percentage of bad reviews. It might be better to stop selling them if they're ruining our brand.

## 5. Is shipping too expensive for our customers?
Shipping in Brazil can be a mess. Sometimes the freight costs as much as the product. I want to find the exact point where shipping becomes so expensive that the customer leaves a bad review, regardless of how good the product is.

## 6. Are we too dependent on one region?
It feels like most of our sellers are in the same few states. If something happens there, the whole business stops. Also, customers far away are probably frustrated with slow shipping. I need to see if we need more sellers in other parts of the country to keep people happy.
